HOW TO ENTER

Ensure that your entries conform to all the rules and are sent in the form stated in the submission formats section.

Please follow the instructions below to enter the Graphic Design Awards Ghana 2015

  • Look at the categories and decide which ones you would like to enter.
  • Please supply the design works and photographs (scanned photographs of published works as proof of publication**) of work in high resolution JPEGS (360dpi). Please note these images may be used for publication and powerpoint show during the event of a nomination and/or win. See below for more specifications stated in submission formats.
  • Entries should be submitted on USB Flash stick (clearly labelled) or on a CD in an A4 envelope containing the ‘individual entry forms’ and clearly labelled.
  • ALL entries MUST be accompanied by a supporting document describing the work.
  • Entries submitted must be published**, displayed or being used by clients.
  • Works submitted should have been designed in the last one year.
  • Please ensure your name or company name is not shown on the design work except self promotion items.
  • Design work entered into more than one category must be treated as a completely separate entry.
  • Entries must be received by no later than 13th February, 2015 by 5pm. Please note that we cannot guarantee that entries received after this deadline will be considered.
  • The judges reserve the right to re-allocate entries that in their view are incorrectly entered.
  • The organisers reserve the right to retain entries for subsequent publication/exhibition in relation to the awards.
  • The organisers reserve the right to verify that all entries have been commercially produced for a commercial client. Proofs of publication or transmission are requested.
  • Unfortunately due to the number of entries received, no correspondence will be entered into regarding entries or feedback given.
  • The judges’ decision is final.
  • Whilst every care will be taken in the storage of entries, the organisers will not accept responsibility for loss or damage of entries.
  • Submissions should be clearly marked.

**Published / Publication - means the design work has appeared in the newspapers or magazine or in case of areas like packaging, call cards, posters currently being used.

Entry Instructions

Main Image

Design works will have to be entered into the awards competition digitally. The following are the general rules you should take into consideration while preparing your main design image.

  1. Size & Resolution: Please make sure the main design image is 3600 x 3600 pixels exactly and save it as 360 dpi jpeg file, high or medium-high quality. (If the image quality is not up to this quality, the image will be disqualified).
  2. Scaling: Do not scale your design more that 150% (1.5X). The image will be pixelated and may not look sharp.
  3. Descriptive Text: Please do not add any watermark to your design image. Descriptions of your design work will have to be supplied on the 'individual entry forms'.
  4. Background Colour: Use white background colour for your image unless the colour of the design work is white, then an appropriate colour should be use as a background colour.
  5. Image Rights: Always use only the images that you have been granted usage of. Do not use other images that you do not have usage right.
  6. Image Colour and Brightness: Submit your design work as colour unless the work is already a black or a gray scale work. Ensure the design work is bright and has been adjusted for contrast.

Supporting Images (Photographs)

The supporting image (photograph) will also have to be entered digitally. A supporting image is a photograph of the final product submitted to give the jury a better picture of how the product is used in the real life. The following are the general rules you should take into consideration while uploading your supporting image.

  1. Photographs: Present professionally taken photographs with better brightness and contrast ratios and are suitable for printed media and broadcast.
  2. Descriptive Text: Please do not add any watermark to your design image. Descriptions of your design work will have to be supplied as a PDF documentation.

Supporting PDF Documentation

All design works should be accompanied by a supporting document otherwise the design work will be disqualified. The following are the general rules you should take into consideration while preparing your supporting PDF documentation.

  1. Language: All the text must be written in simple English. Remember that the jury is international. Do not use local slang or heavy jargon while explaining your design. For any text do not use “ALL CAPITALS”, use “Title Case” instead. Use 11 point Arial font for all text.
  2. Project Name: Include a project name. For example: B Co Ltd., call cards project or Kwaku Paul’s business cards project.
  3. Primary Function: Include a primary function of the design work.
  4. Size of Design Work: Include the dimensions of the work.
  5. Production Technology and Material: Tell the jury about the materials used (e.g recycled ‘egg shell’ paper etc). (200 words)
  6. Unique Properties: What makes your design so unique that you deserve to win? (300 words)
  7. Award Category: Which category does your design work fits best? If you do not choose a category, jury members will assign or suggest the most appropriate category for your submission
  8. Dated, Name Printed and Signed: By your General Manager or Managing Director or as a freelance Graphic Designer
